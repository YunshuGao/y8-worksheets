📊 MARKING RUBRIC OVERVIEW
Understanding the rubric: Your assessment task is marked using a five-level rubric from A (Outstanding) to E (Limited). Read each level carefully to understand what the markers are looking for. Use this to set a target grade and identify what you need to do to reach it.
A
Outstanding
Detailed, thorough work across all sections. Creative and well-communicated design ideas. Excellent model quality.
B
High
Thorough work with clear design communication. Strong model construction with good detail.
C
Sound
Satisfactory work across most sections. Design ideas are communicated adequately. Model is functional.
D
Basic
Some sections incomplete. Design ideas are partially communicated. Model lacks detail.
E
Limited
Many sections incomplete or missing. Minimal design communication. Model incomplete or not attempted.

🟢 Support
  • Compare your work to the grade descriptions above — which one sounds most like your work?
  • Use sentence starters: “I gave myself a ___ because my work shows...”
  • Ask a partner to check your self-assessment and give honest feedback
🟣 Extension

  • Write an action step that would move your work from your current grade to the next level
