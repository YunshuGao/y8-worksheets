📝 PART 1 — What is a Procedure?
A procedure tells someone HOW to do something. It uses action verbs (imperative words) and steps must be in sequential order.
1

2

💪 ACTION VERBS FOR MODEL CONSTRUCTION
Measure
Use ruler to find exact length
Mark
Use pencil to indicate a point
Cut
Separate using scissors/knife
Score
Cut partway for fold line
Fold
Bend along scored line
Glue
Join surfaces with PVA
Align
Line up edges precisely
Assemble
Put parts together in order
⚠ Score ≠ Cut! Scoring means cutting partway through the cardboard to create a fold line. You do NOT cut all the way through!

📏 PART 2 — What is Scale?
Scale is the ratio between the model and real object
1:20  —  1 cm on model = 20 cm in real life
Real → Model: divide by 20   |   Model → Real: multiply by 20
⚠ Two-Step Process: Convert metres to centimetres FIRST, then divide by 20. Do NOT divide metres by 20 directly!
📖 WORKED EXAMPLE — Scale Conversions
Container length: 6.1 m = 610 cm ÷ 20 = 30.5 cm → round to 30 cm
Container width: 2.44 m = 244 cm ÷ 20 = 12.2 cm → round to 12 cm
Container height: 2.59 m = 259 cm ÷ 20 = 12.95 cm → round to 13 cm
