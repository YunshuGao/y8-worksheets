📝 PART 1 — What is a Net?
A net is a 2D (flat) shape that folds up into a 3D object. All faces are connected along fold lines. Small flaps called tabs are added for gluing faces together.

Container Net Dimensions (1:20 Scale)

Bottom (floor)
30.5 cm × 12.2 cm
Front face
30.5 cm × 13 cm
Back face
30.5 cm × 13 cm
Left end
12.2 cm × 13 cm
Right end

Tabs
1.5 cm wide on gluing edges

🏗 PART 2 — Architectural Symbols
Architectural symbols are standardised drawings used in floor plans worldwide. Everyone uses the same symbols so anyone reading the plan understands it immediately. Based on Australian Standard AS 1100.
