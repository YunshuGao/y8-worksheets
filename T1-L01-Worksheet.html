📝 CLASS NOTES — The History of Containerisation

Timeline of Containerisation

Before 1956
Goods were loaded individually — this was called break-bulk shipping.
What were the problems with break-bulk shipping?
1956
Malcolm McLean invented the modern shipping container.
What was McLean’s key idea?
ISO 668 Standard
An international standard ensuring all containers are the same size worldwide.
Why is standardisation important for global trade?
Today
Over 800 million containers exist worldwide; ~50 million in active use.
What happened to shipping costs as a result of containerisation?

How Shipping Containers Are Made

Key fact: Containers are made from Corten steel (weathering steel) that develops a protective rust layer. The walls are corrugated for extra strength.
