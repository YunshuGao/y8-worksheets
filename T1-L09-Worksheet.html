🔄 ISOMETRIC VS ORTHOGRAPHIC
Isometric DrawingOrthographic Drawing
Shows 3D appearanceShows flat 2D views
One drawing shows three facesSeparate drawing for each face
Measurements can be distortedShows TRUE dimensions
Looks realisticShows exact measurements
Good for visualising the designGood for building from
Key point: Engineers and architects need BOTH types. Isometric shows what it looks like. Orthographic shows exact measurements for construction.
👁 THE THREE STANDARD VIEWS
FRONT VIEW
(Elevation)
Looking directly at the front of the container. Shows width and height.
SIDE VIEW
(End Elevation)
Looking at the short end. Shows depth and height.
TOP VIEW
(Plan)
Looking down from above. Shows width and depth (the floor plan!).

📐 THIRD ANGLE PROJECTION LAYOUT
Australia uses third angle projection (also USA and Canada). The layout positions views as if you “unfolded” the object: Top view ABOVE front view. Side view to the RIGHT.

📏 PROJECTION LINES — The Key to Accuracy
Projection lines are light construction lines that connect the same dimension across different views. They ensure that the width in the front view matches the width in the top view, and that the height in the front view matches the height in the side view.

Why are projection lines important in orthographic drawing?
