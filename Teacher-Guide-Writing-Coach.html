Overview & Purpose

What these tools are and why they exist

During Weeks 6–10, students are building their 1:20 scale cardboard models. While you circulate helping with construction, students also need to develop their academic writing skills for the design portfolio (Part A of the assessment).

Two interactive tools have been created to run alongside model construction:

Tool Purpose When
Assessment Writing Coach 6-session scaffolded writing program teaching rubric literacy, ALARM/VSCC framework, paragraph construction, and self-assessment. Builds writing skills progressively for HSC preparation. ~25 min per lesson, self-paced on devices during construction time
Design Portfolio Digital submission template for the assessment task. Students fill in all 4 sections, upload images of drawings and model, complete evaluation cards. Students work on this throughout weeks 5–10, submitting by 25 March
Key design principle: Both tools are self-guided, so students work independently while you focus on hands-on model construction support. Each Writing Coach session takes ~25 minutes, leaving ~50 minutes per lesson for construction.
EAL/D scaffolding built in: Word banks with simple definitions on hover, sentence frames at multiple levels (emerging/developing/proficient), step-by-step paragraph builders, example responses at all grade levels (A through E), connectives wall organised by purpose.

How It Works

Quick overview of the tools’ features

Writing Coach Features

  • 6 tabbed modules — students click through tabs at the top to move between sessions
  • Auto-save — all typed responses save to localStorage every 500ms
  • Progress tracking — green progress bar at the top, and tabs show a green tick when completed
  • Word banks — clickable words with hover definitions; click to copy to clipboard
  • Sentence frames — fill-in-the-blank starters for emerging writers
  • Paragraph builders — step-by-step guided paragraph assembly
  • Example responses — collapsible examples at all 5 grade levels (E through A)
  • Interactive quizzes — verb level matching, ALARM identification
  • Self-assessment checklists — students tick off success criteria
  • Save as PDF — print button generates clean PDF of all work

Design Portfolio Features

  • 4 assessment sections — colour-coded to match assessment rubric
  • Image upload — click boxes to upload photos of drawings, floor plans, and model
  • Scale conversion table — 1:20 ratio practice with editable fields
  • PMI analysis grid — Plus/Minus/Interesting side-by-side
  • Evaluation cards — clickable 1–5 rating buttons for each criterion
  • Final checklist — 17-item pre-submission checklist
  • Progress bar — real-time percentage of fields completed
  • Share Link — encodes all text data in URL for teacher viewing

How Students Share Progress

The share-via-link system explained

Student Steps

1
Student completes some or all of their work in the Writing Coach or Design Portfolio.
2
Student clicks the Share Link button (green button in the top toolbar).
3
The tool generates a URL that contains all their written responses encoded in the link. The link is automatically copied to their clipboard.
4
Student pastes the link into Google Classroom (assignment submission, private comment, or a Google Doc).

Teacher Steps

1
Open the student’s shared link in your browser. All their text responses and ratings will appear pre-filled.
2
Check the progress bar to see overall completion percentage.
3
Review individual responses and provide feedback via Google Classroom.
Limitation — images: Uploaded images (drawings, photos) are stored only in the student’s browser and are NOT included in the share link (too large). Students must upload images separately to Google Classroom. The share link only transfers text, checkboxes, and ratings.
Pro tip: Ask students to share their link at the start of each lesson so you can check who is on track and who needs support. You can open multiple student links in browser tabs for quick comparison.

6-Lesson Schedule Overview

Mapping Writing Coach sessions to lessons T1-L15 through T1-L20

Dual-track structure: Each 75-minute lesson is split into ~25 min Writing Coach (self-guided on devices) + ~50 min model construction (teacher-guided). Students rotate or do Writing Coach first, then construction.
Lesson Week Writing Coach Module Construction Activity
T1-L15 Week 6 Session 1: Understanding Assessment & Rubrics Net construction — cutting cardboard, preparing pieces
T1-L16 Week 6 Session 2: ALARM & Breaking Down Questions Model assembly — folding, taping/gluing walls
T1-L17 Week 7 Session 3: Writing Section 1 (Identifying & Defining) Interior features — counter, equipment, partitions
T1-L18 Week 7 Session 4: Writing Section 2 (Research & Planning) Interior detailing — signage, accessories, finishing
T1-L19 Week 8 Session 5: Writing Section 4 (Testing & Evaluating) Model finishing — final touches, photographing model
T1-L20 Week 8 Session 6: Final Portfolio Check Portfolio completion — Design Portfolio submission prep
Flexibility: If a student finishes their Writing Coach module early, they can start their Design Portfolio or continue model construction. The modules are designed to be self-paced.

Lesson 1 — T1-L15 (Week 6)

Writing Coach Session 1 + Net Construction

Lesson Plan — 75 minutes

Week 6, Lesson 1
0–5 min
Setup: Students open devices and navigate to the Writing Coach link. Project the link on the board. Explain the dual-track structure: “You will spend about 25 minutes working through the Writing Coach on your device, then the rest of the lesson on model construction.”
5–30 min
Writing Coach — Session 1: Understanding Assessment & Rubrics
  • Students read through the rubric explorer (5 grade levels with “I can” statements)
  • Complete the verb quiz (matching verbs to thinking levels)
  • Self-paced — circulate briefly to ensure all students have opened the tool
30–70 min
Model Construction: Cutting and assembling the net
  • Distribute cardboard sheets and tools
  • Students measure 30 cm × 12 cm × 12 cm net using their scale calculations
  • Demonstrate cutting technique — safety with craft knives/scissors
  • Circulate and assist with measurements and accuracy
70–75 min
Pack up & check: Materials stored safely. Quick show of hands: “Who completed Session 1?” Remind students they can revisit at home.
Teacher focus during construction: Accuracy of measurements. Use a ruler to spot-check student nets. This is an engineering unit — emphasise precision.

Lesson 2 — T1-L16 (Week 6)

Writing Coach Session 2 + Model Assembly

Lesson Plan — 75 minutes

Week 6, Lesson 2
0–5 min
Do Now: Students open Writing Coach and click on the Session 2 tab. Write on board: “Today: Learn ALARM & VSCC, then continue building your model.”
5–30 min
Writing Coach — Session 2: ALARM & Breaking Down Questions
  • Students learn the ALARM matrix (Acquire, Link, Analyse, Respond, Make)
  • Practise VSCC breakdown (Verb, Subject, Content, Concept)
  • Complete 2 VSCC practice activities and the ALARM level quiz
  • Mini check-in halfway: ask 2–3 students to explain what VSCC stands for
30–70 min
Model Construction: Folding and assembling walls
  • Students fold the net along scored lines
  • Demonstrate taping/gluing technique for clean joins
  • Cut windows and serving hatch openings
  • Begin constructing the box structure
70–75 min
Exit check: Quick verbal quiz — “What does the V in VSCC stand for?” Pack up materials.

Writing Coach Session 3 + Interior Features

Lesson Plan — 75 minutes

Week 7, Lesson 1
0–5 min
Do Now: Open Writing Coach → Session 3 tab. Also introduce the Design Portfolio link: “From today, you should also start filling in your Design Portfolio — this is what you submit for your assessment.”

Key transition moment: From Lesson 3 onward, students should be transferring their practised writing from the Writing Coach into the Design Portfolio. The Coach is for learning and practising; the Portfolio is the actual submission.

Lesson 5 — T1-L19 (Week 8)

Writing Coach Session 5 + Photographing Models

Lesson Plan — 75 minutes

Week 8, Lesson 1
0–5 min
Do Now: Open Writing Coach → Session 5. Announce: “Today is evaluation day! We will also photograph your models for the portfolio.”
5–30 min
Writing Coach — Session 5: Writing Section 4 (Testing & Evaluating)
  • Students evaluate against 4 criteria (appearance, functionality, sustainability, safety)
  • Rank and justify each criterion using the word bank and sentence frames
  • Write “What I would improve” using the WWW/EBI/YNS framework
  • Transfer evaluation writing into Design Portfolio Section 4
30–55 min
Model photographing: Set up a “photo station” with good lighting
  • Students take photos: exterior, interior (top-down view), floor plan next to model
  • Upload photos directly into the Design Portfolio Section 3 image boxes

55–70 min
Portfolio completion time: Students fill in remaining Design Portfolio fields. Teacher circulates to help with any sections students are struggling with.

Progress check: All students share their Design Portfolio link. Identify students who need catch-up time next lesson.
Photo quality matters: Model photos should be clear, well-lit, and show detail. The floor plan photo is especially important — it must clearly show scale measurements. Consider setting up a simple photo backdrop (white paper on a desk near the window).

Writing Coach Session 6 + Final Submission

Lesson Plan — 75 minutes

Week 8, Lesson 2
0–5 min
Do Now: Open Writing Coach → Session 6 (Final Portfolio Check). Board: “Last chance! Complete your portfolio checklist and submit today.”
5–25 min
Writing Coach — Session 6: Final Portfolio Check
  • Students work through the 24-item portfolio checklist
  • Self-grade each section using the rubric selectors
  • Create a 3-priority action plan for remaining gaps

Portfolio completion and catch-up:

Differentiation & EAL/D Support

How the tools support diverse learners

Feature How It Helps EAL/D Learners
Word banks with hover definitions Students see simple English definitions when they hover over technical words. Reduces vocabulary barriers without simplifying the content.
Sentence frames Pre-structured sentence starters at multiple levels. Emerging writers can use fill-in-the-blank frames; proficient writers can adapt them.
Paragraph builders Step-by-step guided assembly (Step 1: topic sentence, Step 2: supporting detail, etc.). Breaks the intimidating “write a paragraph” task into manageable pieces.
Example responses at all 5 levels Students can see what an E-level vs A-level response looks like. Helps them understand quality expectations and set personal goals.
Connectives wall Organised by purpose (adding, comparing, cause/effect, sequencing, concluding). Students click to copy useful linking words into their writing.
Self-paced modules No time pressure. Students who need longer can revisit at home. Fast finishers move to the next module.
Visual progress tracking Green ticks on completed tabs and progress bars provide motivation and clear sense of achievement.
Emerging learners: Encourage them to use the sentence frames and word banks heavily. Tell them: “It’s OK to use the sentence starters — that’s what they’re there for.” The tool is designed so that even a student using only the frames and word bank can produce a C-level response.
Extension learners: Challenge them to write responses without using the sentence frames, aiming for A-level quality. They can use the example A-level responses as benchmarks.

Marking & Assessment

How to assess student work from the tools

What you mark: The Design Portfolio (submitted via share link + images on Google Classroom) and the physical model. The Writing Coach is a learning tool — you do not need to mark it separately. It prepares students to write better responses in the portfolio.

Assessment Breakdown (25 marks total)

SectionMarksWhat to Look For
Section 1: Identifying & Defining 5 Accurate definition of containers, 4+ structural features identified, sustainability explained with reasoning (not just listed)
Section 2: Research & Planning 5 3 researched restaurants with details, completed PMI, creative design brief response, labelled sketches uploaded
Section 3: Model Construction 10 Accuracy of 1:20 scale, neatness of construction, interior features present, floor plan shows correct scale and labels
Section 4: Testing & Evaluating 5 All 4 criteria evaluated with justified ratings, thoughtful improvement suggestions, genuine reflection on process
Quick marking tip: Open each student’s share link in a new tab. Check the progress percentage first — anything below 70% likely has significant gaps. Use the Design Portfolio’s final checklist to quickly identify missing items.

Troubleshooting

Common issues and solutions

ProblemSolution
“My work disappeared!” Data is saved per-device per-browser. If they used a different computer or browser, their previous work won’t appear. Solution: always use the same device. If they shared their link previously, opening that link will restore text data.
“The link is too long!” The share link encodes all responses as base64 — it can be very long. This is normal. It works fine when pasted into Google Classroom. Students can also use a URL shortener.
“My images didn’t transfer!” By design — images are stored locally only and are too large for URL encoding. Students must upload photos separately to Google Classroom.
“The page won’t load” Check the internet connection. The pages are hosted on GitHub Pages and require internet access. If school network blocks GitHub, try on mobile data.
“I accidentally cleared my work” Unfortunately, “Clear All” is permanent. There is a confirmation dialog, but once confirmed the data is deleted from localStorage. Emphasise to students: the clear button is only for starting completely fresh.
Student can’t access at home The tools work on any device with a web browser (phone, tablet, computer). Share the link via Google Classroom so students can access it easily from home.
Two students sharing a device They should use different browsers (e.g. one uses Chrome, one uses Edge). Or one can save their link, clear the data, then the other works. Not ideal — try to get separate devices.

Reuse for Year 7

How to adapt these tools for your two Year 7 classes

The Writing Coach was designed with reusability in mind. The ALARM/VSCC framework and writing scaffolding methodology are universal — they work for any Technology assessment.

What Stays the Same

  • Sessions 1 & 2 (rubric literacy, ALARM, VSCC) — these are unit-agnostic
  • Word bank structure and sentence frame approach
  • Paragraph builder methodology
  • Self-assessment and checklist framework
  • WWW/EBI/YNS feedback sandwich

What Needs Changing

  • Sessions 3–5 content: Replace container/restaurant vocabulary with Year 7 unit vocabulary
  • Example responses: Rewrite to match the Year 7 assessment questions
  • Rubric content in Session 1: Update “I can” statements to match Year 7 rubric
  • Design Portfolio: Restructure sections to match Year 7 assessment structure
Fastest approach: Duplicate the Writing Coach HTML file, rename it, then use find-and-replace to swap container-specific terms. The structure, styling, and JavaScript can remain identical. Only the text content needs updating.

Since Year 7 students don’t have an assessment this term, you can use these tools purely as writing skill development — focusing on Sessions 1 and 2 (ALARM/VSCC) to build their foundation for future assessments.
