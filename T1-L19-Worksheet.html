🎙 PRESENTATION PREPARATION

Next lesson you will present your container restaurant to the class. Use the prompts below to prepare your talking points.

What is your restaurant called and what is its concept?

e.g. “Flame Box — a gourmet burger bar specialising in flame-grilled patties with locally sourced ingredients.”

Name 2–3 key design features of your container restaurant.

Think about: serving window, awning, layout, sustainable features, branding.

Describe the workflow inside your restaurant (how food moves from delivery to customer).

What was one challenge you faced during the project and how did you overcome it?

What is one important thing you learned during this 10-week unit?

🟢 Support
  • Use the sentence starters provided in each text box
  • Focus on completing the catch-up priority list — evaluation first
  • Presentation can be short — aim for 3–4 sentences covering your restaurant name, one feature, and one thing you learned
🟣 Extension
  • Include sustainability features in your presentation (e.g. solar panels, recycled materials, water recycling)
  • Explain how your design solves a specific problem at festivals or events
  • Compare your design to a real-world container restaurant you researched
  • Prepare a “pitch” as if selling your design to a festival organiser

TermDefinitionMy Example / Notes
Gallery walkA structured activity where students move around the room to view and assess each other’s work
Peer feedbackComments and suggestions given by classmates to help improve work
Two Stars and a WishA feedback strategy: two positive comments (stars) and one suggestion for improvement (wish)
ConstructiveFeedback that is helpful, specific and focused on improvement rather than criticism
PresentationSharing your design work and process with an audience, explaining decisions and outcomes
Transferable skillsSkills learned in one context that can be applied in other situations or subjects
